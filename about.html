Jars of air are a unique and innovative way to preserve and enjoy the freshness of the outdoors. These jars are filled with air from specific locations or natural environments, such as forests, mountains, or beaches. The idea behind them is that by capturing the air from these special places, you can bring a piece of that location into your home and experience the benefits of that air. The air is collected through a process called air harvesting, which involves using a special device to collect the air and then sealing it in a jar. This process ensures that the air remains fresh and pure, free from pollutants and other contaminants. Many people find that jars of air can have a calming and rejuvenating effect, helping to reduce stress and improve overall well-being. Additionally, they make for a great conversation piece and thoughtful gift. All in all, jars of air are a unique and enjoyable way to bring a piece of nature into your home and improve your overall well-being.
Mason jars, also known as Ball jars, are a type of glass jar that were originally used for canning and preserving food. They are named after John Landis Mason, who patented the screw-on lid with a rubber seal in 1858. Mason jars are still commonly used for canning and preserving, as the seal on the lid creates an airtight environment that helps to keep food fresh for a longer period of time. They are also used for other purposes such as storage, decor, and as a drinking glass. The clear glass of Mason jars allows you to see the contents inside and the wide mouth of the jars makes it easy to fill and clean. They are also reusable, durable and can withstand extreme temperatures in the canning process.
